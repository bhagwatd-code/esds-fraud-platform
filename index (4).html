Common Attack Types Targeting Banks

üé£

Phishing & Vishing Attacks

Fraudsters impersonate bank officials via calls/SMS/email to extract OTPs, card numbers, and credentials. Victims are tricked into authorising fraudulent transactions.

High Volume Social Engineering
‚úÖ ESDS detects: Unusual transaction velocity, device mismatch, geo anomaly after credential use
üí≥

Card Skimming & Cloning

Skimming devices planted on ATMs/POS terminals capture card data. Cloned cards are used for fraudulent transactions, often in a different geography from the victim.

High Value Physical + Digital
‚úÖ ESDS detects: Geographic impossibility, spending pattern deviation, new merchant category
üì±

UPI & Mobile Payment Fraud

Fraudulent collect requests, fake QR codes, and screen-sharing scams targeting UPI users. India sees over 50,000 UPI fraud complaints per month.

Fastest Growing UPI / IMPS
‚úÖ ESDS detects: Collect request anomalies, beneficiary account age, transaction timing patterns
ü§ñ

Account Takeover (ATO)

Using stolen credentials from data breaches, attackers log in to customer accounts and rapidly drain funds through multiple small transactions to avoid detection.

High Damage Credential Stuffing
‚úÖ ESDS detects: New device fingerprint, login-to-transaction speed, IP reputation scoring
üè¶

Money Mule Networks

Fraudsters use networks of compromised or recruited accounts to layer and transfer stolen funds, making the money trail difficult to trace for investigators.

High Complexity Organised Crime
‚úÖ ESDS detects: Network graph anomalies, rapid fund movement, beneficiary risk scoring
üí∞

Business Email Compromise (BEC)
